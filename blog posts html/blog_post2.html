Cruise ship: The floating City of Waste

The luxurious pollution

An affordable, luxurious all-inclusive voyage on the ocean, visiting the most beautiful places, on a huge ship with everything you could possible need. Going to sleep after a day packed with entertainment and food, only to wake up at the shores of a new country, with new exciting experiences. Sounds like the perfect escape from every day life.

But to the environment, the cruise industry is a everything but beautiful. During a typical one-week voyage, a large cruise ship (with 3,000 passengers and crew) is estimated to generate 210,000 gallons of sewage; 1 million gallons of graywater (wastewater from sinks, showers, and laundries); more than 130 gallons of hazardous wastes; 8 tons of solid waste; and 25,000 gallons of oily bilge water.
